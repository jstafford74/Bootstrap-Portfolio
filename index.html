20+ years of successful trading and risk management experience spanning a variety of markets structures and environments including open-outcry trading pits, hedge funds and sell-side institutions. My interest in finance grew out of natural curiosity following the American Greetings and Orange County derivative lossess in the mid to late 90's. Following graduation from Boston College, I pursued a career in derivative trading as a floor member of the CBOE and senior trader/risk manager for a large scale equity and index option trading business. Since 2002 my experience and interest in finance resided in credit and credit derivatives, specifically companies rated below investment grade, or 'High Yield'. As the initial trading hire at Lehman Brothers in 2003 for all U.S. High Yield portfolio products and exotic credit derivatives, I was instrumental in formulating and building an extremely profitable customer-facing business that obtained the #1 ranking by Greenwich Advisors at the peak of the financial crisis. Having navigated the risk markets profitably during the financial crisis, especially at a firm central to the root causes, I am featured in the New York Times Best Seller written by Lawrence G. McDonald, 'A Colossal Failure of Common Sense, The Inside Story of the Collapse of Lehman Brothers'. In 2017 I started working in business development and project management with a medical device startup company focused on noninvasive ways to detect blood glucose. Currently I am the business development & project manager for a dermatologist practice in Stamford, CT focused on noninvasive solutions to treat skin cancer. I hold FRM and PMP certifications, my series 7 & 63 FINRA registrations, am a licensed benefits advisor in the state of CT for health and accident insurance, and working to obtain the MatLab professional certification in February 2020. In addition I am expected to complete the UConn Full-Stack Engineering program in January 2020 offered through Trilogy Education/2U.
